Understanding Industrial Pressure Units

Pressure is force per unit area. In SI units, it's measured in Pascals (Pa). However, because a Pascal is very small, we often use Bar (100,000 Pa). In countries like the USA, PSI (Pounds per Square Inch) is the standard for hydraulics and tire pressure.

Torque and Mechanical Advantage

Torque (Nm) is a measure of the force that can cause an object to rotate about an axis. It's critical in engine design and gear system analysis. Converting between Newton-meters and other units ensures compatibility with international machinery standards.
