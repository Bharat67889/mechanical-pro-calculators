Understanding Pressure Units: Pascal vs. Bar vs. PSI

Pressure is defined as force per unit area. In the International System of Units (SI), the Pascal (Pa) is the standard. However, because one Pascal is a very small amount of pressure, engineers often use Bar (100,000 Pa) for industrial applications.

  • Pascal (Pa): The base SI unit. Used in fluid mechanics and structural analysis.
  • Bar: Common in weather forecasting and automotive tire pressure.
  • PSI: Predominantly used in the United States for hydraulic systems and air compressors.

Torque Calculations: Nm and kgf-m

Torque is the rotational equivalent of linear force. For mechanical engineers designing gearboxes or engines, Newton-meter (Nm) is the primary unit. However, older machinery manuals often use kgf-m. Our tool ensures that your torque specifications meet the required standards without manual error.
