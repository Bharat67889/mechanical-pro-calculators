Understanding Gear Ratios in Mechanical Engineering

A gear ratio is the ratio of the number of rotations of a driver gear to the rotations of a driven gear. It is one of the most fundamental concepts in Theory of Machines and mechanical design. Using our Gear Ratio Calculator, students can quickly find the mechanical advantage provided by different gear sets.

The Simple Formula

To find the gear ratio, you simply divide the number of teeth on the driven gear (T2) by the number of teeth on the driver gear (T1). For example, if you have a 40-tooth driven gear and a 20-tooth driver gear, the ratio is 2:1.

Why Is It Important?

Engineers use gear ratios to control speed and torque. A higher ratio increases torque but decreases speed, which is essential in heavy machinery and automotive transmissions. This tool helps in designing gearboxes, bicycles, and even robotic arms.
