Understanding Gear Ratio in Mechanical Systems

Gear ratio is one of the most fundamental concepts in Mechanical Engineering and Automotive Design. It determines how power is transmitted between two rotating gears. Whether you are building a simple toy or a complex industrial gearbox, knowing the ratio between the Driver Gear and the Driven Gear is essential.

The Gear Ratio Formula

The calculation is simple but powerful. The formula used in our calculator is:

Gear Ratio = Number of Teeth on Driven Gear / Number of Teeth on Driver Gear

Speed vs. Torque Trade-off

In mechanics, you never get something for nothing. When you use gears to increase Torque (by having a larger driven gear), you automatically decrease the Speed (RPM). Conversely, to increase speed, you need a smaller driven gear, which results in lower torque. This is known as mechanical advantage.
